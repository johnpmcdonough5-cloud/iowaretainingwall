Retaining Wall Materials Compared

Six common retaining wall materials, side by side, so you can match the right one to your budget, slope, and style.

Published July 14, 2026 · 8 min read

Choosing a retaining wall material is a trade-off between cost, lifespan, appearance, and how much load the wall has to hold back. There's no single "best" material — there's the best material for your slope, soil, and budget. Here's an honest comparison of the six we build most often across Eastern Iowa.

1. Segmental concrete block

Interlocking block walls are the workhorse of modern hardscaping. They install quickly, flex slightly with Iowa's freeze-thaw cycles, and come in dozens of colors and textures. Expect 40+ years of life with almost no maintenance. Best for: most residential walls up to 6 feet.

2. Poured concrete

Poured concrete walls are the strongest option and ideal for tall, structural, or load-bearing situations. They can be finished smooth or faced with stone veneer. Best for: engineered cantilever walls and walls holding significant grade.

3. Brick

Brick walls deliver a timeless, traditional look that complements older homes. Brick is durable and low-maintenance but sits at the higher end of the price range. Best for: homeowners prioritizing classic curb appeal.

4. Natural stone & stone veneer

For the premium, organic look, nothing beats stone. Full natural stone is the costliest option, while stone veneer over a concrete core delivers the same appearance for less. Best for: feature walls and high-visibility front yards.

5. Treated timber

Wooden walls are the budget choice with a warm, natural feel. The trade-off is lifespan: even pressure-treated timber typically lasts 15–20 years in Iowa's wet, freeze-thaw climate. Best for: short walls and tight budgets.

6. Gabion

Gabion walls — wire cages filled with rock — offer excellent natural drainage and a rugged, modern look. They handle water beautifully, which suits Iowa's clay soils. Best for: drainage-heavy sites and contemporary landscapes.

Quick comparison

  • Longest lifespan: Poured concrete & stone
  • Best value: Segmental block
  • Lowest upfront cost: Timber
  • Best drainage: Gabion
  • Most classic look: Brick & natural stone

Not sure which fits your yard? We'll walk your property, factor in slope and soil, and recommend the material that gives you the best long-term value.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the best material for a retaining wall?

For most Eastern Iowa homes, segmental concrete block offers the best balance of cost, durability, and appearance. For tall or structural walls, poured concrete is stronger; for premium looks, stone or brick win. The right choice depends on wall height, drainage, and budget.

Which retaining wall material lasts the longest?

Poured concrete and natural stone walls last the longest - often 50 to 100 years when properly drained. Segmental block commonly lasts 40-plus years. Treated timber is the shortest-lived at roughly 15 to 20 years in Iowa's freeze-thaw climate.

Are gabion walls good for Iowa's climate?

Yes. Gabion walls drain freely, so water doesn't build up behind them - an advantage in Iowa's clay-heavy soils. They also flex with freeze-thaw movement rather than cracking, making them a durable, low-maintenance option.
