Gravity retaining wall design and installation

Gravity retaining walls rely on their own weight and mass to resist the lateral pressure exerted by the soil. They are ideal for areas that require minimal to medium support. If a property is heavily sloped or requires holding back large amounts of soil, a gravity wall may not be the most suitable option compared to other wall types.

Our team has been providing gravity retaining wall services for many years. We emphasize the importance of professional construction to prevent issues like wall overturning, base sliding, or soil-bearing capacity failures.

Key advantages of gravity retaining walls

Gravity walls are one of the oldest and most proven types of retaining wall. Here are some of the top reasons property owners choose gravity walls:

  1. 1.   Simple and proven design

    Gravity walls rely on their sheer mass and weight to hold back soil, making them one of the simplest and most time-tested wall designs available. There are no complex mechanical parts or anchoring systems required.

  1. 2.   Wide material options

    Gravity walls can be built using a variety of materials including natural stone, concrete blocks, brick, and gabion. This flexibility allows property owners to match both the functional needs and the aesthetic style of their property.

  2. 3.   Ideal for minimal to medium support

    Gravity walls work best for areas requiring minimal to medium soil support. For properties with mild slopes or shallow retaining needs, they provide a reliable and cost-effective solution without the complexity of other wall types.

  3. 4.   Professional installation prevents failure

    Even with a simpler design, professional construction is critical for gravity walls. Poor installation can lead to wall overturning, base sliding, or soil-bearing capacity failures. Our experienced team ensures every wall is built correctly from the ground up.
