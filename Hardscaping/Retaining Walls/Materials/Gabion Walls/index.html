Why are they so popular?

When compared to other materials that can be used for retaining walls such as wood and stone, bricks are not really a cheap alternative at around $10 to $45 per square foot. Why are many people still adamant about using them?

Retaining walls made of gabion are:

  1. 1.   Easy to work with

    If you are looking for a material that will not set you back a lot when it comes to your budget, gabion is a popular choice.

    Gabion does not take a lot of money to transport and is easier to install compared to other materials. Hence, gabion retaining walls are budget-friendly for many locals who need functionality.

  1. 2.   Faster to complete

    Remember when we said that retaining walls made of gabion are easier to work with? This usually results in a fast turnaround time.

    Due to the reason that retaining walls are often urgent due to erosion and flooding issues, some clients would prefer walls that are fast to complete. Most often, the materials that are necessary to make gabion walls are just wire mesh, loose rocks, and stones. These materials are usually accessible and easy to find in most cities in the United States.

  2. 3.   Best for those with drainage issues

    Although it may take time to get used to the appearance of gabion walls, there is no doubt that they can do their job effectively. Gabions are durable and can withstand moisture, which makes them a valuable partner in flood-prone areas.

  3. 4.   Sustainable and flexible

    In the present, it is important for many people to use environment-friendly solutions as much as possible. Aside from the reason that gabion retaining walls prevent erosion and flooding, they also leave less carbon footprint than other materials.

    When gabions are highly functional in both rural and urban areas, it is unsurprising why many people want them.
