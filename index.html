Types of Backyard Walls

Walls can be built using a wide range of materials, each one having its pros and cons. Here are some of the more popular materials to consider:

Concrete Retaining Wall

Concrete retaining walls are highly durable with resisting soil and water pressure. They offer versatility in design and finishes, serving both functional and aesthetic purposes. Low-maintenance and cost-effective, they prevent erosion, create level spaces, and improve the stability and appearance of your landscape.

Stone Retaining Wall

Stone retaining walls are an excellent choice for landscaping due to their exceptional durability and long-lasting performance. Made from natural stone, these walls can withstand the pressures of soil and water, making them highly effective at preventing erosion and ensuring the stability of your property.

Railroad Tie Walls

Railroad tie retaining walls are a cost-effective and practical addition to any landscape. These durable, weathered wood ties provide reliable soil support, preventing erosion and creating level garden beds or pathways. Easy to install, they're ideal for budget-friendly landscaping solutions.

Block Walls

Block walls are an excellent option for landscaping, offering easy installation and versatility. Available in a range of sizes, shapes, and colors, they can be customized to fit any design preference. These walls provide reliable support for soil retention and help prevent erosion.

Frequently Asked Questions

How Much Does a Retaining Wall Cost?

Retaining wall costs vary based on height, length, materials, site access, drainage requirements, and local regulations. We provide free on-site estimates so you get an accurate quote for your specific project before work begins.

Do retaining walls need drainage?

Yes. Proper drainage is critical to retaining wall longevity. Without it, water pressure builds behind the wall, which can cause bulging, cracking, or complete failure. Our team designs drainage into every wall we build.

How long does a retaining wall last?

A properly designed and installed retaining wall can last decades. Lifespan depends on materials, drainage, soil conditions, and maintenance. Professional installation is the best way to maximize your wall's durability.
